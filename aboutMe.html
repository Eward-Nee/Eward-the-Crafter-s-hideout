About me:

When I first started using a Computer:

Well, since the first day I remember, I already used a computer. When asking my father and mother, they say that I started using a Computer when I was 2 years old. I played puzzle and math games. I remember that I played almost the entire day when I was six, that is when I was not at school. In Grade 4 I learned that my primary school give programming lessons after school. Sadly we could not affort it. In Grade 5 I got a game idea that I wanted to program, but I did not know how. I guess it was eather in grade 4 or 5 when a switch was triggered that made me want to learn coding. In grade 7 I stoped playing games that can keep me busy for more that a hour a day.

My subject choises:

At the end of grade 9 all the grade 9's got a letter that was filled with small tick boxes in which you must mark what subject you want to take in grade 10. I went with math, eng FAL, afrikaans home, physical science, accounting and last, but not least IT(computer science).

Grade 10 and 11:

From grade 10 I knew I was going to do good in IT. I would listen for a couple of seconds what the teacher sait, then I would ignore the rest and figure it all by my self out. I enjoyed going home and making programs for the fun of it. I also made a few games. On test day I showed up with methods and algorithms I figured out on my own that works for solving commen problems found in grade 10 and 11 tests. All was going very good untill the first test I could not get done in time. I am a slow typing.

Grade 12:

I dont know why, but the firts time I started to learn IT by doing past papers was for the grade 12 end exam and damn. That made a big difference. The test was as easy as can come. Sadly the start of the year was horrible.

My struggles and solutions:

I have my fair share of problems. I can't go to university. I don't have a turor. I am not sure that I am learning the right stuff, because everyone have their own fullstack roadmap. I don't have money to be able to get certificates online. All this makes me stress, but...
I have a solution. Just try over and over again to become a web-developer. One day it will work. It may sound stupid, but atleast it is something. I will close my eyes, pray and try.

My vision:

I believe that everyone that wants ro reach success must have a plan to be successful.

Now this is my plan:
1. Learn web development.
2. Get a web development job.
3. Work hard.
